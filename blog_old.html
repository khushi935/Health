TeleICU & Acute Care

The TeleICU/Acute Care committee provides a stabilizing influence for organizational concepts and directions to be established and maintained with a visionary view for the TeleICU/Acute Care environment. The TeleICU/Acute Care SIG may collaborate with other SIGs on key aspects of driving telehealth forward within the acute care space

Telemedicine and Remote Care

Traditional medical practice is not always the most efficient or convenient way to provide care to our neurology patients. With an increasing demand for neurologic services in a growing population, technology can be one way to extend our reach to our patients. As technologies develop, it is paramount that practitioners maintain high-quality care, equivalent to traditional in-person visits..

Physician shortages in Rural hospitals

A recent study report, Of the 25 respondent facilities (74% response rate), 96% reported vacancies that resulted in staffing difficulties within the last 3 years and 84% relied on intermittent providers to staff their inpatient services in the last year. Almost two‐thirds of respondents thought that a telehospitalist service could help address their staffing needs and 72% were interested in participating in a pilot program.

Telepsychiatry

The use of video-based telepsychiatry helps meet patients’ needs for convenient, affordable and readily-accessible mental health services. With a robust evidence base that shows telepsychiatry leading to improved outcomes and higher patient satisfaction ratings, policy makers, payers and providers are increasingly considering ways to implement and use telepsychiatry.
